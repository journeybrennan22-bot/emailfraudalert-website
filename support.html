Frequently Asked Questions

What email platforms do you support?
EFA works with Gmail (via Chrome extension) and Microsoft 365/Outlook (via Office add-in). For Outlook, you'll need a Microsoft 365 subscription — older or on-prem versions aren't supported.
Does EFA read my email content?
No. Your email stays private.

EFA analyzes technical signals — sender address, domain, reply-to fields, routing patterns. Your email content is processed locally and never leaves your device. We don't read your emails. We don't store them.
What is a "lookalike email"?
An email that looks right — until you look closely. One letter changed. One character added. A slightly different domain. Easy to miss.

EFA checks those details automatically — so you don't have to.
How do I install EFA?
For Gmail: Install from the Chrome Web Store and grant access to your contacts.

For Outlook: Install from Microsoft AppSource — the add-in appears in your Outlook ribbon.
Does EFA guarantee I won't be defrauded?
No tool catches everything. But EFA is built to catch what actually causes losses — before you act.

It detects the patterns used in real-world fraud, at the moment it matters most. For anything involving money, always verify independently.
Why did I get a warning on a legitimate email?
Because scammers use the same patterns as legitimate emails. That's the reality.

EFA is designed to give you a moment to pause — not to block you. If everything checks out, move forward. That pause is the protection.
