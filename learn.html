6 Tactics. Millions of Victims.

Every email scam uses the same playbook. Learn to recognize the tricks — or let us catch them for you.

1

Lookalike Email Addresses

One character. Total impersonation.

Criminals create email addresses nearly identical to someone you trust. A single letter difference is all it takes to steal everything.

Examples
support@chase.com → support@chasse.com
john.smith@company.com → john.smith@cornpany.com
escrow@abctitle.com → escrow@abctitIe.com
Home buyers Business owners Employees Everyone
2

Reply-To Hijacking

Your reply goes to them, not who you think.

The email looks legitimate. But when you hit "Reply," your response goes to a completely different address controlled by the attacker.

How It Works

From: ceo@yourcompany.com

Reply-To: ceo-yourcompany@gmail.com

You think you're emailing your CEO. You're emailing a criminal in another country who now has whatever you sent them.

Executives Finance teams Assistants Anyone
3

Dangerous Keywords

The language of fraud.

Fraud emails use specific phrases designed to create urgency and bypass your critical thinking.

Red Flags

"Verify your account immediately"

"Updated wire instructions"

"Unusual sign-in activity detected"

"Your account will be suspended"

"Confirm your identity"

"Action required within 24 hours"

Bank customers Online shoppers Account holders Everyone
4

Deceptive Domains

Familiar names, criminal origins.

Criminals register domains that look like trusted companies by exploiting international extensions and common typos.

Fake Domains
amazon.com.co — Not Amazon
paypal-secure.com — Not PayPal
apple.support-id.com — Not Apple
irs.gov.tax-refund.com — Not the IRS
Online shoppers Taxpayers Tech users Everyone
5

Display Name Spoofing

The name you trust, the address you ignore.

Email programs show the display name prominently and hide the actual address. Criminals exploit this to impersonate anyone.

What You See vs. Reality

In your inbox: "Bank of America Security"

Actual address: xr77281@randomdomain.com

In your inbox: "Dr. Smith's Office"

Actual address: notreal@spamserver.net

Patients Clients Customers Everyone
6

Invisible Character Tricks

Identical to your eyes. Different to computers.

Some foreign alphabet letters look identical to English letters but are technically different characters. Perfect visual forgeries.

Can You Tell the Difference?
microsoft.com (Latin letters)
micrоsoft.com (Cyrillic 'о')

Your eyes cannot detect this. Only software can.

Tech companies Banks Government Everyone
